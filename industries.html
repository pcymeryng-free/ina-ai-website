Industries

Ten sectors. One common language of bankability.

Digital infrastructure is no longer just telecom. It spans the physical and computational layers that power modern economies.

Telecommunications

Fixed and mobile operators expanding coverage, upgrading networks and structuring next-generation licensing and spectrum strategies.

Fiber Networks

Backbone and last-mile fiber deployment — route economics, rights-of-way, open-access models and financing structures.

Mobile Networks

4G/5G rollout planning, tower and RAN sharing structures, and regulatory strategy for national mobile coverage.

Data Centers

Hyperscale and edge data center siting, power availability, financing and public-sector co-location strategy.

Cloud Infrastructure

Sovereign and commercial cloud strategy, digital public infrastructure hosting, and cloud procurement structuring.

Satellite Systems

LEO/GEO connectivity strategy, spectrum coordination and satellite-terrestrial hybrid network structuring.

AI Infrastructure

Compute capacity planning, power and cooling strategy, and public-private structuring for national AI infrastructure.

Submarine Cables

Route planning, landing station strategy, capacity offtake structuring and multi-country consortium financing.

Smart Cities

Digital public services, IoT infrastructure and integrated urban connectivity strategy.

Energy

Power availability strategy for digital infrastructure, and structuring of energy-digital co-investment.
