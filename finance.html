Financing Landscape

Multilateral Finance, in one map

Development finance institutions increasingly prioritize projects that combine public policy objectives with private investment. INA speaks that language on both sides of the table.

  • Multilateral Development BanksLong-tenor, policy-aligned financing for public infrastructure.
  • Development Finance InstitutionsBlended mandates across development and commercial return.
  • Project FinanceNon-recourse structures tied to asset cash flows.
  • Public-Private PartnershipsRisk-shared delivery across public and private balance sheets.
  • Blended FinanceConcessional capital that de-risks commercial participation.
  • GuaranteesCredit enhancement that unlocks otherwise unbankable projects.
  • Export Credit AgenciesFinancing tied to cross-border equipment and services.
  • Commercial & Institutional CapitalBanks and investors once a project clears readiness.
